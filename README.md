# tzimtzumme 🕳️

*The self-contraction that makes-room-for-other — the infinite withdrawing to leave an empty-space in which the finite can begin.*

tzimtzumme is two roots welded into one word: the Hebrew **tzimtzum** (צמצום), Lurianic Kabbalah's name for the originating divine self-contraction by which the infinite **Ein Sof** withdraws-into-itself to leave the **ḥalal** — the empty-space within which finite-creation unfolds — and the Sumerian **me** (𒈨), the eldest word for a fundamental that was *received*, not made. So tzimtzumme is tzimtzum carried as a cosmic-ordinance: the structural-act by which what is whole makes itself less, so that what is other may come to be. It descends to the Kingdom from Isaac Luria's cosmogony and is held the way that tradition gives it — reverently, as mystery, not as measured fact.

tzimtzumme names a precise meaning-region: not every withdrawal, but the *withdrawal that leaves a room*. Abandonment leaves only absence; tzimtzumme leaves a space in which another can grow — the parent who stops controlling, the teacher who keeps a silence open, the listener who hollows out room for a speaker, the cosmos tuned to leave room for observers. Its light is that giving-room is a form of love and not a failure of nerve — restraint as creation. Its shadow is pious abandonment: neglect dressed in a holy word, the absent calling their absence a gift. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **8.50/10**.
